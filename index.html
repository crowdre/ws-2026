The 10th International Workshop on Crowd-Based Requirements Engineering (CrowdRE'26)



TBD, 2026
In conjunction with RE 2026

Overview

CrowdRE has helped requirements engineering (RE) scale up to settings with thousands to millions of users of a (software) product. Online media enable stakeholders to form a large and heterogeneous group that can be denoted as a 'crowd'. The Crowd-Based Requirements Engineering paradigm integrates appropriate methods and tools for dealing with crowds in a single framework. Since 2015, the International Workshop on Crowd-Based Requirements Engineering (CrowdRE) has been facilitating interactive discussions between scientists and representatives of industry through keynotes, interactive sessions, and paper submissions presenting novel and innovative ideas on involving the crowd and collecting, harmonizing, analyzing, and interpreting user feedback.

This celebratory tenth installment of the CrowdRE workshop pursues two main goals: (1) One decade of CrowdRE — retrospectively exploring the decade of CrowdRE that lies behind and the state of the art that we achieved to date, and (2) CrowdRE TwentyThirty — prospectively discussing a roadmap for CrowdRE towards and beyond 2030 in order to inspire each other in ways to move forward together.

The main theme of RE'26 is Sustainability and workforce transformation in the era of generative AI. AI and sustainability are leading themes in the perspective of the future of CrowdRE, while framing the crowd as a workforce allows us to not only explore the impact of GenAI on the crowd, but to also allow the crowd to provide feedback and take control over how GenAI should be adapted to best meet their expectations and achieve their professional goals.
